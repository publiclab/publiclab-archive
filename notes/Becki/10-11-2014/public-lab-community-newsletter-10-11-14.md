In the Public Lab Community Newsletter this week, vote for the Homebrew Oil Testing Kit at Engadget.com’s Insert Coin competition and check out a great series of notes on Sensor Journalism from Emerson College students.

**Announcements**

The Homebrew Oil Testing Kit has been chosen as one of the 16 contenders for Engadget.com's Insert Coin competition -- and now there's a public vote for who will make it to the top 10 semi-finalists. Votes will be accepted until October 15th, 2014, midnight PST. Please support Public Lab's entry and support the development of affordable pollution testing! [Vote here](http://insertcoin.engadget.com/)! 

Kickstarter Updates! The [DIY Tar Sands Kit](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/publiclab/the-homebrew-oil-testing-kit/posts/998956) has been a community favorite!  

The Fall Public Lab Barnraising will be November 14-16 in Cocodrie, LA. Save the date and [sign up today](http://publiclab.org/wiki/barnraising)!  

**Upcoming events**

As part of The Hacktory’s fall workshop series, Sean McGinnis will be [leading a workshop (in Philadelphia) on October 18th](http://publiclab.org/notes/SeanKMcGinnis/09-18-2014/balloon-mapping-raspberry-pi-workshop-the-hacktory) on the basics of balloon, kite and pole mapping and using Raspberry Pi as a sensor base while mapping.

Mark your calendar for the next Toolshed raising Saturday, December 6 at Parts and Crafts in Somerville, MA, featuring short presentations, open working time, and workshops on Public Lab projects and anything related. RSVP [here.](http://publiclab.org/notes/Hudonnoodles/10-10-2014/toolshed-raising-on-saturday-december-6)

**New and ongoing projects**

Students from [MAST @ FIU BBC](http://mastfiu.dadeschools.net/), a public high school that’s part of the Miami-Dade County Public Schools and housed at Florida International University’s Biscayne Bay Campus, with the support of [FIU’s School of Journalism and Mass Communication](http://journalism.fiu.edu/), spent Wednesday morning building Public Lab’s [Coqui](http://publiclab.org/wiki/coqui) sensors that they will use for their upcoming King Tide Day events on Alton Road in Miami Beach on Oct. 9. Read more [here.](http://www.eyesontherise.org/sensors2/)
