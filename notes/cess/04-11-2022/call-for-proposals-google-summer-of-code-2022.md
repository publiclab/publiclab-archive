# Call for proposals:  Google Summer of Code 2022 

### Google Summer of Code(GSoC) 2022

We are participating in Google summer of code, we have been  participating in Google summer of code for a while now.

> Google Summer of Code is a global, online mentoring program focused on introducing new contributors to open source software development. GSoC contributors work on a 12+ week programming project with the guidance of mentors from their open source organization.

We're asking students to start posting their proposals for comment, here. Read on!

### Projects

 - [Idea list/ potential projects](https://publiclab.org/wiki/gsoc-ideas#2022+Ideas)
 -  [Github discussion](https://github.com/publiclab/plots2/issues/10754)

### Deadline

GSoC applications will be close on April 19, 2022.

### Read proposal ideas

Once you've gotten a bit familiar with our project (see [our welcome page here](https://code.publiclab.org))
Please feel free to ask questions in the comments below, or to join our chat channel at [https://publiclab.org/chat](https://publiclab.org/chat)

Our welcoming page will help you understand how we collaborate, and we'll be there to help you take your first step. And [try tackling a first-timers-only issue](https://code.publiclab.org/#r=all), to get an idea of how to become a contributor!

![image description][1]

Also, read this page carefully to understand our workflow and how we structure summer of code projects: [https://publiclab.org/wiki/gsoc#How+we+work](https://publiclab.org/wiki/gsoc#How+we+work)

Please ask questions! We're very friendly and we love welcoming new people into our community.

### [Post a proposal for comment](https://publiclab.org/notes/warren/02-28-2019/call-for-summer-of-code-20120-proposals#Post+a+proposal+for+comment)

Once you've read through some of these ideas and become a bit more familiar with our projects, it's time to post a proposal of your own.

You don't need to have a complete or even concrete idea -- share your thoughts early and we'll help you refine your proposal, and help you choose something that's the right difficulty level as well as being something our project really needs.

We've created a template for you to post a proposal here. *Please note:the format for proposal is a little different for GSoC this year, please feel free to format the template to the new format.*  
Before you get started because of a  common question often asked  - **you will still have to post the pdf proposal on the Google Summer of Code website for you to be eligible for the internship selection**
