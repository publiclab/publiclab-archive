# Public Lab Community Newsletter June 14, 2014

by [Shannon](../profile/Shannon) | June 15, 2014 15:41

June 15, 2014 15:41 | Tags: [newsletter](../tag/newsletter), [newsletter-archive](../tag/newsletter-archive)

----

This week in the Public Lab community newsletter take a look at images from the Barnraising last weekend, read updates on the final Barataria Bay mapping trip and work being done around dust sensing and check out posts on community science and storytelling such as “exploring the space unseen”. Enjoy!

**Announcements**

The Public Lab nonprofit is excited to Welcome Natalie Mayorga to the staff. Natalie is joining us as the Civic Kits Manager and will be working from our Portland, OR office. Read more about Natalie [here](http://publiclab.org/profile/Natalie) and get get in contact with Natalie by emailing Natalie@publiclab.org. 

The [Infragram Point and Shoot camera](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/publiclab/infragram-the-infrared-photography-project/posts), is now available for pre-order! Order yours today by visiting the Public Lab store: [http://store.publiclab.org/collections/diy-infrared-photography/products/infragram-point-shoot-plant-cam](http://store.publiclab.org/collections/diy-infrared-photography/products/infragram-point-shoot-plant-cam ) 

Last weekend, around thirty people got together for the first Spring tool-focused Barnraising and the first Organizers Summit. This year, the gathering was held in Plymouth, MA. Keep an eye out for more Barnraising notes to come, but here’s a couple from Chris Fastie: [http://publiclab.org/notes/cfastie/06-11-2014/ne-barnraising-2014](http://publiclab.org/notes/cfastie/06-11-2014/ne-barnraising-2014) and [http://publiclab.org/notes/cfastie/06-10-2014/ne-organizers-summit](http://publiclab.org/notes/cfastie/06-10-2014/ne-organizers-summit). Eymund Diegel also shared a series of photos: [https://www.flickr.com/photos/11001189@N07/sets/72157645114965494/ ](https://www.flickr.com/photos/11001189@N07/sets/72157645114965494/ )

Save the Date for the Annual Fall Barnraising in Cocodrie, Louisiana. We’ll be convening this year from November 14-16.

Interested in using the IRC chat feature on Public Lab to communicate with staff, organizers and other community members? Here’s a note on how to jump into the conversation: [http://publiclab.org/notes/anishshah101/06-05-2014/how-to-access-the-public-lab-internet-relay-](http://publiclab.org/notes/anishshah101/06-05-2014/how-to-access-the-public-lab-internet-relay-)chat 

The shiny new Infragram interface has been ported to the faster, WebGL version of the site which can do plant analysis NDVI on live video: [http://infragram.org/sandbox/?webgl=true](http://infragram.org/sandbox/?webgl=true)

**New and ongoing projects**

The Barataria Bay, Louisiana project is wrapping up the mapping phase. Check out a note from Scott Eustis, with additional comments by Dan Beavers, on the work they’ve been doing: [http://publiclab.org/notes/eustatic/06-13-2014/barataria-2014-vii-june-12th ](http://publiclab.org/notes/eustatic/06-13-2014/barataria-2014-vii-june-12th )

Updates and progress on the thermal fishing bob from a [test at a power plant](http://publiclab.org/notes/Sara/06-13-2014/successful-thermal-fishing-bob-test-mapping-the-kendall-power-plant-thermal-plume) and from the [Barnraising last week](http://publiclab.org/notes/Kaya9804/06-09-2014/thermal-fishing-bob-presentation-from-the-ne-barnraising%20). 

Hagit Keysar shares her proposal for the (accepted) talk that she will be giving at the Open Hardware Summit in Rome this year on open sourcing the architect’s model: [http://publiclab.org/notes/hagitkeysar/06-12-2014/civil-architecture-and-open-hardware](http://publiclab.org/notes/hagitkeysar/06-12-2014/civil-architecture-and-open-hardware)

Mathew Lippincott has shared a great series of research notes on the work that he has been doing around dust monitoring over the last two weeks. This work is in preparation for a project on sand frac mining in Wisconsin. Check out the notes by looking under the “Open Air” tag: [http://publiclab.org/tag/openair](http://publiclab.org/tag/openair).

Anishshah101 provided two research notes updating on the progress of Google Summer of code work. Read more about working on [image distortion in MapKnitter](http://publiclab.org/notes/anishshah101/06-13-2014/gsoc-update-working-of-image-distortion-in-mapknitter) and [non-affine/projective transformations of images](http://publiclab.org/notes/anishshah101/06-02-2014/gsoc-update-leaflet-draw-and-non-affine-transformations). 

Eymund Diegel discusses how historic resources, such as maps, can help guide future storytelling in his note on “exploring the space unseen”: [http://publiclab.org/notes/eymund/06-10-2014/exploring-the-space-unseen-historic-resources-for-guiding-future-story-telling](http://publiclab.org/notes/eymund/06-10-2014/exploring-the-space-unseen-historic-resources-for-guiding-future-story-telling ) 

Public Labber, kanarinka, provides a walk through of an unnamed neighborhood creek and thoughts on how nearby residents set out to learn more about this body of water: [http://publiclab.org/notes/kanarinka/06-09-2014/the-neighborhood-creek-expedition](http://publiclab.org/notes/kanarinka/06-09-2014/the-neighborhood-creek-expedition) 

Website tips: pages can now have "parent pages" which lead back to a top-level wiki page. See an example: [http://publiclab.org/wiki/infragram-convertible-cameras](http://publiclab.org/wiki/infragram-convertible-cameras)
