Join us for a series of events the week of December 13, when we'll be wrapping up our [latest research area review](https://publiclab.org/notes/bhamster/10-18-2021/delving-into-air-quality-data-for-our-next-research-area-review) and talking about **all things air quality data**. Events will include:

- a presentation on getting started with visualizing and communicating air quality data, followed by a community discussion,
- a guest speaker session featuring community scientists working with air quality data,
- a live build of Public Lab's Simple Air Sensor

**Details and call links for each event are below!**

## üì£ Community scientists working with air quality data: guest speaker session

**When**: **Thursday, December 16, 12pm PT / 2pm CT / 3pm ET / 8pm UTC / [other time zones](https://everytimezone.com/s/33545f7b).** This event will run for approximately 1 hour.

**Where**: <a class="btn btn-primary" href="https://zoom.us/j/93236546515?pwd=MlljcUFIcnd6S0QrUTNOVG1BbTRqdz09">Air Quality Community Projects: Guest Speaker Session</a>

**What**: The following speakers will share their information, updates, and lessons learned from their air quality monitoring projects.

 - **Mary Jo Burke**, resident of the Lower East Side of Manhattan, in New York City who, along with other residents, is concerned about the air quality in the neighborhood, especially as it relates to the destruction of the East River Park. Her presentation will be about LES Breathe, a volunteer run group that was created within the organization East River Park Action with public health, transparency and awareness as goals, that developed a citizen science project to monitor air quality in the neighborhood.
 - **Christian Torres**, Special Projects Manager overseeing the Identifying Violations Affecting Neighborhoods (IVAN) network, air monitoring efforts, citizen science projects, and research collaborations for Comite Civico del Valle. Christian also coordinates policy advocacy with their team & external partners across California.

## Who are these events for?

**Anyone who's interested in communicating environmental data.** We're focusing on air quality data but a lot of what we'll be talking about applies to any kind of environmental data. People who are looking for resources and a supportive community might find this call especially helpful.
