# Mount the Camera

1. Attach 3D printed camera holder to baseplate using M3 bolts(16mm)**![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/703/large/assembly_1.jpg "assembly_1.jpg")**
2. Attach camera to holder (NOTE: These M3 bolts were too large and I had to drill out the camera mounting holes -- should get smaller bolts for future versions)![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/705/large/assembly_4.jpg "assembly_4.jpg")
3. Attach optics mount to camera bottom ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/706/large/assembly_5.jpg "assembly_5.jpg")

---------

# Build the Stand

1. Attach back-holder to baseplate![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/707/large/assembly_8.jpg "assembly_8.jpg")
2. Attach back to back-holder with slide (20mm bolts + washers) so it can slide but stays in place ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/708/large/assembly_10.jpg "assembly_10.jpg")
3. Screw in objective to set table height. Objective tip should be at roughly the same height as top of table. You can get this precisely with combination of slide and notch height. ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/709/large/assembly_11.jpg "assembly_11.jpg")
4. Attach table ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/710/large/assembly_12.jpg "assembly_12.jpg")
5. Add feet -- feet elevate above screws and dampen vibration ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/712/large/assembly_13.jpg "assembly_13.jpg")

---------

# Attach the Camera

1. Plug ribbon into camera ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/714/large/assembly_14.jpg "assembly_14.jpg")
2. Plug ribbon into pi![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/713/large/assembly_15.jpg "assembly_15.jpg")
3. Connect the pi to laptop via USB to provide power ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/715/large/assembly_16.jpg "assembly_16.jpg")

---------

# Attach the Focus Bolt

1. The carriage bolt acts as a lead screw. Rotate it through the hole and screw it down until it touches the base plate![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/716/large/assembly_17.jpg "assembly_17.jpg")
2. Hold sample plate in tension with rubber band![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/717/large/assembly_18.jpg "assembly_18.jpg")

---------

# Build a Light 

You'll need a light source to illuminate your slides -- one that you can position precisely. We'll show you how to build one here the way we often do with kids at Parts & Crafts, but you can also [buy a light to use](https://publiclab.org/notes/stevie/01-22-2018/what-is-the-best-light-source-to-use-for-the-basic-microscope). Tell us what works best for you!

# ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/725/large/lighting_assembly.jpg "lighting_assembly.jpg")

  
  
1. Extend the wires on the battery pack. The wires coming off of the battery pack are not quite as long as we would like them to be so we start by attaching extra lengths of wire. If you have a soldering iron you should solder these connections. Do this for both the red and black wires. ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/718/large/lighting_2.1.jpg "lighting_2.1.jpg")
2. Attach the LED. Red wire goes to the long lead, black wire goes to the short lead ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/720/large/lighting_3.png "lighting_3.png")
3. Glue to coathanger wire. This provides a flexible mount for your light ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/721/large/lighting_4.png "lighting_4.png")![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/722/large/lighting_5.png "lighting_5.png")
4. Bend the wire to position the light. It should point directly down, facing into the objective lens. ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/723/large/lighting_6.png "lighting_6.png")
5. Take some pictures -- use binder clip to hold slide in place and bolt for focus adjustment. Make your light is pointing directly into lens to get enough light. ![image description](https://publiclab.org/system/images/photos/000/022/726/large/IMG_20171124_184509_%283%29.jpg "IMG_20171124_184509_(3).jpg")
