##What and Why<br>

We in the [clean gulf commerce coalition](http://cleangulfcommercecoalition.org/united-bulk-fouls-the-mississippi-river/) want to do quick test of coal or petroleum coke bits that coal export terminals for certain metals, like selenium. <br><br>

##My attempt and results<br>

Half a mile from the discharge of [United Bulk](http://cleangulfcommercecoalition.org/gulf-export-facilities/oiltanking-united-bulk-terminal/), Devin Martin and Scott Eustis went to the river ([29.61438,-89.882922](http://goo.gl/maps/Wq9vB)) a half mile from an [unpermitted discharge](http://publiclab.org/notes/eustatic/05-28-2013/kite-photos-of-ongoing-coal-pollution-in-plaquemines-parish-la), and got some coal, which is not supposed to be there.<br><br>

##Questions and next steps

For the next steps, I'm looking to do the green lazer test, the UV lamp test, and shoot a spectra of the coal or petroleum coke chunks. 

The spectrum will hopefully help determine whether or not there are heavy metals in the solvent, or in the coal chunk. I'm going to start with Selenium, as per this [research note](http://publiclab.org/notes/warren/08-13-2013/importing-spectra-from-nist-and-webmineral-com-to-spectral-workbench).
