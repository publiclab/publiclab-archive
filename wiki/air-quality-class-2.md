# Air Quality Class 2

by [jmsaavedra](../profile/jmsaavedra), [liz](../profile/liz)

February 11, 2013 22:01 | Tags: [epa](../tag/epa), [cypress-hills](../tag/cypress-hills), [chaq](../tag/chaq)

<h3><a href="/wiki/air-quality-class">Class Home</a></h3>

<h3>Getting Started</h3>

<strong>Prior to class:</strong> Facilitator reviews blog posts before class, and identifies 5 - 10 great research site proposals.

<strong>In class activity A:</strong> Sensor location review

* Review the student postings on publiclaboratory.org, 
* Recap what makes the sites suitable or not, 
* Discuss viability of sites in terms of physical access & power. 

<strong>In class activity B:</strong> Close look at the sensor units

* Point out what each component is measuring 
* Explain how each component works 
* Explain how data is being recorded, and the clock. 

<strong>In class activity C:</strong> Assemble one unit

* Plug in the power jack, 
* Insert the SD card, 
* Screw the board into the housing.
* Review what is needed to install a sensor unit properly.

<strong>In class activity D:</strong> Class installs and connects multiple (2 - 5) units in the classroom or just outside of the classroom, for practice and experience.

<h3>Week 2 Assignment:</h3> 
<strong>Take home sensor units</strong>

* 6 students in each class (12 total) are chosen to take home sensor units in the first round 
* In class, specify on <a href="http://airqualityegg.com">airqualityegg.com</a> where the sensors will be installed
* Students bring sensors home and install them in their homes
* These 12 students make posts on the blog detailing where they installed their sensor
* Other students make blog posts recapping what they learned in class

<strong>Post includes:</strong>

* 1 image of their sensor unit plugged in and turned on
* At least 2 images of what is <em>around</em> the sensor
* Writing about what challenges they encountered 
* Writing other observations

<strong>Sensors will remained installed for 2 weeks in this location.</strong>
