<p>Public Lab is a community where you can learn how to investigate environmental concerns. Using inexpensive DIY techniques, we seek to change how people see the world in environmental, social, and political terms.</p>

<p>Community members develop tools and write open source instructions as <a href="/notes">research notes</a>, wiki pages, <a href="/guides">printable guides</a>, and <a href="/videos">videos</a>, so others know how to use these tools, and many have <a href="/wiki/creating-local-mailing-lists">started local groups</a> in their area. We also provide support through our <a href="/wiki/mailing-lists">mailing lists</a>. This works because everyone <b>both learns from and helps each other out</b>, and we all contribute to the growing body of documentation as we go.</p>

<h2><a href="/user/register">Join now</a> to take part!</h2>

<p>This community began as the <a href="http://grassrootsmapping.org">Grassroots Mapping project</a>, an effort to produce Do-It-Yourself satellite imagery <a href="/tool/balloon-mapping">with balloons and kites</a>, most notably during the 2010 BP oil spill. We are now broadening our scope to explore new inexpensive and community-led means to measure and explore environmental and social issues.</p>
