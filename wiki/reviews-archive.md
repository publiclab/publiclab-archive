### December 13-17, 2021: Air Quality Data research area review

### December 16, 2020: Air quality research area review 

Wrapping up Public Lab‚Äôs latest research area review on air quality, we held a virtual get-together on community air monitoring. We saw highlights of updated air quality resources on the Public Lab website, talked about local air monitoring projects across the US, and shared goals, questions, and tips with each other.

Joining us to share their ongoing projects and experiences in community air monitoring:

+ [@MsSema](https://publiclab.org/profile/MsSema) and [@mashalawais](https://publiclab.org/profile/mashalawais), responding to the ITC fire in the Houston, TX area, by developing lower-cost, real-time monitors and a mobile alert system for benzene and other emissions from petrochemical facilities.
+ Deborah Mills, Wendy Brawer, Veronica Olivotto, and Mary Jo Burke of [LES Breathe](https://eastriverparkaction.org/breathe/): developing a community-run air monitoring, reporting, and remediation network in the Lower East Side of New York City.
+ Gustavo Aguirre Jr., Kern County Director at the [Central California Environmental Justice Network](https://ccejn.org/), with experience in Bucket Brigades, driving changes in air quality policy in California, and engaging youth in air quality monitoring.
+ [@eustatic](https://publiclab.org/profile/eustatic) of [Healthy Gulf](https://www.healthygulf.org/), focusing on particulate matter monitoring and interested in ways to prevent community burnout in the face of chronic issues, plus immediate remediation as part of FEMA responses.  
+ [@kgradow1](https://publiclab.org/profile/kgradow1), [@jjcreedon](https://publiclab.org/profile/jjcreedon), and Ethan McMahon of the US EPA, also joined the call to share questions and ideas.
